FAIR and FAIRification

FAIR(Findability, Accessibility, Interoperability, and Reusability) principles serve to guide data producers and data publishers to improve their data management practices and improve the reuse of research data[1] FAIRification is the process of improving the FAIR level of datasets, projects or related infrastructure.

The FAIR wizard utilizes FAIRification resources developed by the FAIRplus project and other platforms, suggests FAIRification materials based on the FAIRification requirements, and designs FAIRification solutions for data owners, data stewards, and other people involved in FAIRification.

How to use FAIR wizard

FAIRification resources

  • Recipes. FAIRification guidelines in the FAIR Cookbook[2] developed by the FAIRplus project[3]
  • FAIRification processes developed by the FAIRplus project.
  • FAIR indicators from the RDA data maturity model[4].
  • Curated tools for FAIRification, sourced from Bio.tools[5].

Curated FAIRification goals and Solutions

The wizard also contains manually curated solutions for common FAIRification use cases.
